𝑟=𝑑−2(𝑑⋅𝑛)𝑛r=d−2(d⋅n)n

where 𝑑⋅𝑛d⋅n is the dot product, and 𝑛n must be normalized.

Let 𝑛̂ =𝑛‖𝑛‖n^=n‖n‖. Then 𝑛̂ n^ is the vector of magnitude one in the same direction as 𝑛n. The projection of 𝑑d in the 𝑛n direction is given by proj𝑛𝑑=(𝑑⋅𝑛̂ )𝑛̂ projnd=(d⋅n^)n^, and the projection of 𝑑d in the orthogonal direction is therefore given by 𝑑−(𝑑⋅𝑛̂ )𝑛̂ d−(d⋅n^)n^. Thus we have

𝑑=(𝑑⋅𝑛̂ )𝑛̂ +[𝑑−(𝑑⋅𝑛̂ )𝑛̂ ]d=(d⋅n^)n^+[d−(d⋅n^)n^]
Note that 𝑟r has −1−1 times the projection onto 𝑛n that 𝑑d has onto 𝑛n, while the orthogonal projection of 𝑟r onto 𝑛n is equal to the orthogonal projection of 𝑑d onto 𝑛n, therefore
𝑟=−(𝑑⋅𝑛̂ )𝑛̂ +[𝑑−(𝑑⋅𝑛̂ )𝑛̂ ]r=−(d⋅n^)n^+[d−(d⋅n^)n^]
Alternatively you may look at it as that −𝑟−r has the same projection onto 𝑛n that 𝑑d has onto 𝑛n, with its orthogonal projection given by −1−1 times that of 𝑑d.
−𝑟=(𝑑⋅𝑛̂ )𝑛̂ −[𝑑−(𝑑⋅𝑛̂ )𝑛̂ ]−r=(d⋅n^)n^−[d−(d⋅n^)n^]
The later equation is exactly
𝑟=−(𝑑⋅𝑛̂ )𝑛̂ +[𝑑−(𝑑⋅𝑛̂ )𝑛̂ ]r=−(d⋅n^)n^+[d−(d⋅n^)n^]

Hence one can get 𝑟r from 𝑑d via

𝑟=𝑑−2(𝑑⋅𝑛̂ )𝑛̂ r=d−2(d⋅n^)n^
Stated in terms of 𝑛n itself, this becomes
𝑟=𝑑−2𝑑⋅𝑛‖𝑛‖2𝑛r=d−2d⋅n‖n‖2n

I was trying to understand how to calculate the reflection vector and found these answers. I couldn't understand them easily, so I took my time to do it myself, the good thing is that I can now detail it in an ELI5 fashion!

I did develop the formula using the 3 steps shown in the graphic. I describe them bellow.

So, the initial situation is 𝑎⃗ a→ pointing toward a plane. Then we have the normal 𝑛⃗ n→ of unit lenght and we would like to find 𝑏⃗ b→

So, the first step is using the dot product to get a vertical vector that will be used in step 2.

With step 1 my partial formula is: 2×(𝑎+(−𝑎⃗ )⋅𝑛⃗ ×𝑛)2×(a+(−a→)⋅n→×n)

mind the change of sign of 𝑎⃗ a→ above, we "flipped" it

Then in step 2, I can write: −𝑎⃗ +2×(𝑎+(−𝑎⃗ )⋅𝑛⃗ ×𝑛)−a→+2×(a+(−a→)⋅n→×n)

Now, I can distribute: −𝑎⃗ +2×𝑎⃗ +2×(−𝑎⃗ )⋅𝑛⃗ ×𝑛−a→+2×a→+2×(−a→)⋅n→×n

Then simplify, and I end up with: 𝑎⃗ +2×(−𝑎⃗ )⋅𝑛⃗ ×𝑛a→+2×(−a→)⋅n→×n

If you negate a vector in the dot product, you negate the result of the dot product.

𝑎⃗ ⋅𝑏⃗ =−(−𝑎⃗ )⋅𝑏⃗ a→⋅b→=−(−a→)⋅b→

That means that I can rewrite the formula like this:

𝑎⃗ −2×(𝑎⃗ )⋅𝑛⃗ ×𝑛a→−2×(a→)⋅n→×n

Suppose that 𝑑d and 𝑟r have the same magnitude.

‖𝑟‖=‖𝑑‖‖r‖=‖d‖
From the reflection relationship, we have this equality about cross products.
𝑟×𝑛 = 𝑑×𝑛∴ (𝑟 −𝑑)×𝑛 = 0⃗ r×n = d×n∴ (r −d)×n = 0→
 which means
𝑟 −𝑑 =𝑠 𝑛∴ 𝑟 = 𝑑 +𝑠 𝑛r −d =s n∴ r = d +s n
 where 𝑠s is a real number.
Taking their squares, we have
‖𝑟‖2 = ‖𝑑‖2+ 2 𝑠(𝑑⋅𝑛) +𝑠2 ‖𝑛‖2∴ 𝑠(𝑠 ‖𝑛‖2+ 2 (𝑑⋅𝑛))=0‖r‖2 = ‖d‖2+ 2 s(d⋅n) +s2 ‖n‖2∴ s(s ‖n‖2+ 2 (d⋅n))=0
 So
𝑠 =0 ,−2 (𝑑⋅𝑛)‖𝑛‖2s =0 ,−2 (d⋅n)‖n‖2
 Since 𝑠=0 s=0  means  𝑑  d  itself, we take the other value and get
𝑟 = 𝑑−2 (𝑑⋅𝑛)‖𝑛‖2 𝑛r = d−2 (d⋅n)‖n‖2 n
